The Evolution of AI

From foundational breakthroughs to the latest insights on reasoning limitations

2017

Attention Is All You Need

Vaswani et al., Google Brain/DeepMind
Introduced the Transformer architecture, revolutionizing NLP by replacing RNNs and CNNs with self-attention mechanisms. This paper laid the foundation for all modern large language models.
Foundational Breakthrough
2018

BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers

Devlin et al., Google AI
Demonstrated the power of bidirectional training and transfer learning, achieving state-of-the-art results across multiple NLP benchmarks and establishing pre-training as the dominant paradigm.
Architecture Innovation
2019

Language Models are Unsupervised Multitask Learners (GPT-2)

Radford et al., OpenAI
Showed that scaling language models leads to emergent capabilities across tasks without explicit training, introducing the concept of zero-shot task transfer and demonstrating the power of scale.
Scaling Breakthrough
2020

Language Models are Few-Shot Learners (GPT-3)

Brown et al., OpenAI
Demonstrated that massive scale (175B parameters) enables in-context learning and few-shot performance, marking the beginning of the large language model era and changing AI's trajectory.
Paradigm Shift
2022

Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback

Ouyang et al., OpenAI
Introduced InstructGPT and RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback), showing how to align language models with human preferences and create more helpful, harmless, and honest AI systems.
Alignment Breakthrough
2022

Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models

Wei et al., Google Research
Showed that prompting models to generate intermediate reasoning steps dramatically improves performance on complex reasoning tasks, launching the field of reasoning enhancement techniques.
Reasoning Innovation
2023

Lost in the Middle: How Language Models Use Long Contexts

Liu et al., Stanford University
Revealed that language models struggle to effectively use information from the middle of long contexts, showing that simply expanding context windows doesn't guarantee better performance. Challenged the assumption that more tokens automatically lead to better AI capabilities.
Reality Check
2024

o1: Learning to Reason with LLMs

OpenAI Research Team
Introduced reasoning models that spend more compute at inference time, showing dramatic improvements on mathematical and scientific reasoning tasks through reinforcement learning on reasoning traces.
Reasoning Models Era
2025

The Illusion of Thinking: Understanding the Strengths and Limitations of Reasoning Models

Shojaee et al., Apple Machine Learning Research
Challenges the reasoning capabilities of LRMs, showing they collapse on high-complexity tasks and rely more on pattern matching than true reasoning. Reveals three performance regimes and fundamental scaling limitations.
Reality Check
